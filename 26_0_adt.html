Here is a chart showing the distribution of water on Earth.

In the first column, notice that 2.5% of Earth's water is freshwater - the amount needed for life to survive.

The middle column shows the breakdown of freshwater. Almost all of it is found in ice and in the ground.

Only a little more than 1.2% of all freshwater is surface water, which provides most of life's needs.

The right column shows the breakdown of surface freshwater.

Most of the surface freshwater is found in ice, and another 20.9% is found in lakes.

Rivers make up 0.49% of surface freshwater.

Although rivers account for only a small amount of freshwater, rivers are the source of most usable water.
